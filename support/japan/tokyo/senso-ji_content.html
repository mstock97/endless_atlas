An In-Depth Guide to Senso-ji Temple and Asakusa Area (Walking Route and Tips)

Senso-ji temple is an absolute must-visit on any trip to Tokyo. This Buddhist temple is the oldest temple in Tokyo and has been a significant place of worship throughout the city’s history. The temple’s origin is believed to begin in 628, when two fishermen brothers found a statue of Avalokitesvara Bodhisattva (also called Kannon in Japanese), a bodhisattva known for mercy and compassion. The temple continued to grow in both size and prominence over the centuries and became a place of worship for the Shoguns, including those from the Tokugawa Shogunate. As Tokyo (formerly known as Edo) evolved into a significant population and government center by the 19th century, Senso-ji continues to be an important center of worship for people throughout Japanese as well as visitors from around the world.

Kaminari-mon Gate

This is the main entrance gate and the traditional starting point for visiting the temple. The gate is flanked by depictions of the gods of wind and thunder, meant to provide protection for the temple. As you pass through the gate, it’s believed that touching the wooden dragon carving on the underside of the red lantern will bring good fortune.
