• 💴 Cash is (Still) King: While credit cards are becoming more common, many local restaurants, shrines, and smaller shops are cash-only. 7-Eleven stores have incredibly tourist-friendly ATMs (Seven Bank) that reliably accept most international debit cards 24/7.
  • 🚆 Get a Digital IC Card: Skip the paper tickets. Load a digital Suica or Pasmo card directly into your Apple Wallet or Google Pay. You can tap your phone for subways, buses, convenience stores, and even vending machines.
  • 🗑️ Carry a Small Trash Bag: Public trash cans are surprisingly rare in Japan. It is customary to hold onto your rubbish until you return to your hotel or find a designated bin (often found next to vending machines or inside convenience stores).
  • 🤫 Transit Etiquette: When riding the subway or Shinkansen (bullet train), keep your phone on silent and strictly avoid taking phone calls. Quiet, respectful travel is the norm.
  • 📶 Connectivity is Crucial: Navigating the complex transit systems without data is tough. Book a Pocket Wi-Fi or download a travel eSIM before you land so you have instant access to Google Maps and translation apps right off the plane.
