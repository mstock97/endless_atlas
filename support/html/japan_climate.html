The best time to visit Japan is generally May, June and October, when conditions across most of the country are at their most comfortable. Japan spans a large area with considerable regional climate differences - in any given month temperatures can vary by more than 13°C between regions, so conditions depend heavily on where you are travelling. The country experiences dramatic seasonal swings. August sees country-wide averages around 84.4F (29.1C) while January brings averages down to around 44.8F (7.1C) - with extremes well beyond these figures in some regions. Year-to-year variability is notable: in a warm August country-wide averages can reach 84.4F (29.1C), while a cool August may only average 84.4F (29.1C). January averages range from 43.9F (6.6C) in a cold year to 45.7F (7.6C) in a mild one. Rainfall is typically highest around June and lowest in January. If possible, avoid travelling in January, March and November, when weather conditions across the country are at their worst. This summary draws on data from Tokyo, Osaka, Sapporo, Fukuoka and Naha.
