Japan is a study in beautiful contradictions. Bullet trains whisk you past centuries-old temples, neon cityscapes dissolve into serene bamboo forests, and the world's most meticulous cuisine is served in both Michelin-starred dining rooms and bustling underground izakayas.

From the electric pulse of Shibuya to the meditative stillness of Kyoto's Fushimi Inari, every corner of Japan rewards the curious traveler with something genuinely unexpected. Traveler Tips
  • Japan is still predominantly cash-based. Carry yen -- ATMs at 7-Eleven and Japan Post are foreigner-friendly.
  • Get an IC card (Suica or Pasmo) at any major station -- it works on trains, buses, and convenience stores nationwide.
  • Buy a JR Pass before you leave home; it cannot be purchased in Japan at the discounted tourist price.
  • Tipping is not customary and can be considered rude. Excellent service is simply the standard.
